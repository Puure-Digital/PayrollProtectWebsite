How The Estimate Works

01 You enter your total payroll spend

Weekly or monthly — your choice. This is the gross payroll you're processing across subcontractors and agency workers on site. Not headcount, not day rates: total spend.

02 We apply a discrepancy rate to your spend

A discrepancy is the gap between what's claimed on a timesheet and what was actually worked. The UK construction average sits at around 4%, sourced from HMRC Tax Gap Statistics and CIPP benchmarking data. High-risk sites — those running multiple agencies, high turnover, or paper-based registers — typically land at 7–8%.

03 The output is your exposure, not confirmed fraud

This figure is what you're statistically likely to be overpaying based on sector-wide data. Some of it is deliberate fraud. Some is administrative error. Some is supervisors signing off what they didn't verify. The cause matters less than the outcome: once those invoices are paid, that money doesn't come back.

04 PayRoll Protect eliminates the discrepancy at source

Rather than auditing after the fact, PPP verifies attendance in real time — biometric, timestamped, site-gated. Discrepancies surface before payment approval, not after legal action starts. The goal isn't to recover money already lost. It's to stop it leaving in the first place.

Common Questions

It's a sector-level estimate, not a site-specific audit. The 4% industry average is derived from HMRC Tax Gap Statistics and CIPP benchmarking data — it reflects the mean across the UK construction sector, which skews toward larger multi-subcontractor operations.

Your actual exposure will depend on how many agencies you use, the sophistication of your current sign-off process, and how long discrepancies have gone unchecked. The calculator gives you a starting point — a real PayRoll Protect assessment gives you the actual figure.

Any gap between what a subcontractor or agency claims on a timesheet and what can be independently verified on site. This includes ghost clocking (workers claiming hours they weren't present for), early departures billed as full days, duplicate billing across agencies, and inflated headcounts on gang-rate submissions.

Administrative errors — supervisors signing off sheets without checking — account for a significant portion. Intent is irrelevant to the financial outcome: the invoice still clears at the wrong figure.

Post-payment audits surface discrepancies after the money has left. Recovery requires disputes, legal action, or writing the amount off. The construction industry standard is to write it off — the cost and friction of recovery typically exceeds the recovered sum, especially when spread across multiple subcontractors.

PayRoll Protect operates at the point of claim, not after payment. Discrepancies are flagged before any payment decision is made, with biometric-backed timestamps rather than someone's signature.

Most sites are operational within a single day. The pilot programme is designed around a 90-day onboarding window: install on one site, generate verified attendance data from your own operation, and use that evidence internally before rolling out across the portfolio.

You don't need to overhaul existing payroll systems. PPP sits alongside your current process and flags discrepancies before the approval step — no rework, no disruption to what already runs.

Use Conservative (3%) if your sites have strong supervisory oversight, few agencies, and existing digital sign-off tools. If you're unsure, start with the industry average. It's the number that tends to hold up in practice, and it's the figure most commercial directors find credible when presenting to boards.
